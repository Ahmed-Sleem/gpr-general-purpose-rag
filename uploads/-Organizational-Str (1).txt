## 1. Introduction to the Guide

This guide serves as a reference for the organizational structure, job responsibilities, and key performance indicators for all positions within Kayan Al-Mamlaka Company. It aims to clarify reporting lines, responsibilities, and performance measurement criteria to ensure operational efficiency and strategic alignment.

---

## 2. Purpose of the Guide

The purpose of this guide is to:

- Define the organizational structure and reporting relationships
- Clarify job responsibilities for each position
- Establish key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure performance
- Provide a reference for recruitment, evaluation, and training decisions
- Ensure alignment with the company's strategic objectives

---

## 3. Scope of Application

This guide applies to all employees, departments, and functions within Kayan Al-Mamlaka Company. It serves as an official reference for organizational matters and shall be used in all administrative and operational decisions related to human resources and performance management.

---

## 4. Administrative Escalation Base

The administrative escalation process follows these principles:

1. Employees shall first address issues with their direct supervisor
2. If the issue cannot be resolved at the direct supervisor level, it shall be escalated to the department manager
3. If the issue is related to cost, time, risks, contracts, or clients, the relevant department such as PMO, Finance, Legal, or QHSE shall be involved
4. If the issue is high-impact or strategic, it shall be escalated to the CEO
5. In matters that substantially affect the company's obligations, reputation, or strategy, the CEO shall escalate to the Board of Directors when necessary

---

## 5. Board of Directors

### 5.1 Board of Directors

**Supervises:** CEO

#### 5.1.1 Job Description

- The Board of Directors holds the ultimate responsibility for the strategic direction and governance of the company, ensuring the achievement of its long-term objectives in the interests of shareholders and stakeholders.
- **Reporting Line:** Represents the highest authority in the company's structure, and the CEO reports to the Board.

#### 5.1.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### 15.7 Specialist Technician

#### 15.7.1 Job Description

- The technician performs specialized technical work in their field (electrical, mechanical, welding, HVAC, etc.) according to approved technical specifications, working under the supervision of the Site Engineer or Site Supervisor.
- **Reporting Line:** Reports to the Site Supervisor or Site Engineer.
- **Requirements:** Diploma or professional certification in the relevant specialization; minimum 2 years of field experience; ability to read basic technical drawings.

#### 15.7.2 Tasks and Responsibilities

- Perform specialized technical work according to approved drawings and specifications
- Conduct initial inspections and tests of equipment and systems
- Report any technical issues to the Site Supervisor immediately upon discovery
- Maintain and care for tools and equipment used
- Comply with quality standards and safety procedures in all work
- Participate in inspection and handover activities with QA/QC team
- Document completed work daily and submit reports to supervisor
- Train new workers on basic tasks when needed

#### 15.7.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Calculation Method | Target |
|-----|-------------------|--------|
| First-Time Work Quality Acceptance Rate | (Accepted work ÷ Total executed work) × 100 | ≥ 90% |
| Rework Rate | (Rework hours ÷ Total work hours) × 100 | ≤ 5% |
| Daily Work Report Compliance | (Reports submitted ÷ Work days) × 100 | 100% |
| Work Safety | Number of technician-related incidents | Zero incidents |

### 15.8 Worker

#### 15.8.1 Job Description

- The worker performs basic manual and operational tasks at the project site, supporting execution teams and technicians in completing work according to direct supervisor instructions.
- **Reporting Line:** Reports to the Site Supervisor.
- **Requirements:** No specific educational qualification required; ability to perform manual work; commitment to safety instructions.

#### 15.8.2 Tasks and Responsibilities

- Perform excavation, backfilling, and manual handling work according to supervisor instructions
- Assist technicians in preparing work sites and arranging materials and equipment
- Maintain site cleanliness and organization during and after work hours
- Move materials and tools between different site areas as needed
- Wear Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) at all times
- Immediately report any hazards or incidents to the direct supervisor
- Participate in safety awareness and training programs
- Perform any other tasks assigned by the direct supervisor

#### 15.8.3 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Calculation Method | Target |
|-----|-------------------|--------|
| Daily Productivity | (Actually executed quantities ÷ Planned quantities) × 100 | ≥ 85% |
| Attendance and Discipline Compliance | (Actual attendance days ÷ Scheduled work days) × 100 | ≥ 95% |
| Safety Standards Compliance | Number of safety violations recorded against the worker | Zero violations |
| Work Quality | Number of positive observations from supervisor on work quality | 2 observations/month |

## 17. Organizational Recommendations

### 17.1 Separation between PMO and Project Management

There should be a distinction between the role of the PMO and the executive project management function:

**PMO Role:** Regulatory and supervisory, including:
- Planning
- Reporting
- Risk management
- Cost control
- Claims
- Standardizing project management methodologies

**Project Management Role:** Executive and operational, including:
- Sector management
- Project managers management
- Execution teams management
- Work execution and project delivery
- Daily client relationship management during execution

### 17.2 Coordination Mechanism Between Departments

The following periodic coordination meetings should be established:
- PMO with Project Management
- PMO with Finance
- PMO with Legal and Contracts
- Business Development with Engineering, Finance, and Operations
- QHSE with Projects and Operations
- Weekly coordination meeting between PMO and Project Managers to review work progress
- Implement an ERP system or unified project management program to improve information flow
- Establish an internal communication platform to enhance inter-departmental coordination
